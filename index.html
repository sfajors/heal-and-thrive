Who We Are

“This isn't just my story — it's the story of far too many Black women in America.”

My name is Kalisha. I'm a 31-year-old woman from Chicago and a survivor of gun violence. In April 2024, I was shot five times. Two bullets struck my aorta. I survived multiple surgeries and lost my left leg above the knee.

The shooter was the younger brother of my childhood best friend — a tragedy rooted in jealousy, trauma, and the systemic issues that plague our inner-city communities.

I received no Victims Fund support, no city or disability housing assistance. So I created Heal and Thrive Chicago — a platform and support system for survivors like me.

Mission & Vision

Our purpose, our plan, and the path we walk together.

Mission Statement

To support and empower Black survivors of gun and domestic violence who have become disabled, and all amputees, by offering physical recovery programs, emotional and mental health services, and pathways to financial independence.

Vision
  • • Create safe, active healing spaces
  • • Break cycles of retaliation and hopelessness
  • • Promote strength, recovery, and reintegration
  • • Begin support at hospital discharge through collaboration with doctors and social workers

Why Now

Over 80% of nonfatal shootings in Chicago affect Black communities. Many survivors are left disabled, unsupported, and forgotten.

Heal and Thrive Chicago was built to change that. We believe survivors deserve more than silence — they deserve a chance to rebuild with dignity, community, and opportunity.
